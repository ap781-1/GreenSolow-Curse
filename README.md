This repository contains research materials exploring the relationship between natural resource abundance, institutional quality, and economic growth. The project extends the traditional Solow growth model by incorporating natural resources and institutional effectiveness to analyze the "resource curse" phenomenon—why some resource-rich countries grow slower than resource-scarce ones.

The core contribution is an augmented Green Solow model that introduces an institutional quality parameter (E) representing the effectiveness of natural resource use, demonstrating how strong institutions can transform a resource curse into a blessing.

Research Question
How does the quality of political institutions influence the impact of natural resource abundance on economic development in resource-rich countries?
